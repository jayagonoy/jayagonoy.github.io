**Art Exhibit featuring Hokusai’s Manga and how it shaped the industry as we see it now**

![](https://jayagonoy.com/wp-content/uploads/1_ImnNb3qY7bIO9WCpaw-kPA.jpg)

Just a week before ToyCon, I visited the Ateneo Art Gallery to witness the “Manga Hokusai Manga” exhibit presented by the Japan Foundation, Manila with support from Embassy of Japan, Manila.

![](https://i2.wp.com/jayagonoy.xyz/wp-content/uploads/1_988Hk96na2V-ZyN84mtmZg.jpg?ssl=1)

![](https://i2.wp.com/jayagonoy.xyz/wp-content/uploads/1_omGhbqpVVhQ3sfOeJJ4InQ.jpg?ssl=1)![](https://i0.wp.com/jayagonoy.xyz/wp-content/uploads/1_R80Z746Y1LGbAGUlqTCKLw.jpg?ssl=1)

![](https://i1.wp.com/jayagonoy.xyz/wp-content/uploads/1_dFy6eQAjCmoH-MCzIxx_zA.jpg?ssl=1)

![](https://i2.wp.com/jayagonoy.xyz/wp-content/uploads/1_Yvv9iyHvlvY6vP6bEhczVg.jpg?ssl=1)

All that I know of Hokusai is the famous painting, which is actually a part of his “Thirty-Six Views of Mount Fuji” series, but I didn’t know that he has done something beyond that. The exhibit chronologically shows the works of Hokusai, as well as contemporary works.

![](https://i0.wp.com/jayagonoy.xyz/wp-content/uploads/1_p9pH58Hh6a8T8w5tvr0eZA.jpg?ssl=1)

![](https://i1.wp.com/jayagonoy.xyz/wp-content/uploads/1_3OtgWlNHJNNC4jt4ENFFoQ.jpg?ssl=1)![](https://i0.wp.com/jayagonoy.xyz/wp-content/uploads/1_B-GY_FY64qSifbejS7FHeg.jpg?ssl=1)

The Hokusai Manga features more than 4,000 sketches and includes instructions on how to draw them. From what I’ve learned, he’s been observing life and animals and flowers so he can draw them right on.

![](https://i0.wp.com/jayagonoy.xyz/wp-content/uploads/1_oMNB1s7hfAIOJN4sBHm_Wg.jpg?ssl=1)![](https://i0.wp.com/jayagonoy.xyz/wp-content/uploads/1_s5c4vOtFtnvE8B37pXfuOg.jpg?ssl=1)![](https://i0.wp.com/jayagonoy.xyz/wp-content/uploads/1_oJF2-RXbl8lldJ_YGJ4K1g.jpg?ssl=1)
