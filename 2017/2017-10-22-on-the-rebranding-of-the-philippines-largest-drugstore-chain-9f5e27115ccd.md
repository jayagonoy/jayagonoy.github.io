### On the rebranding of the Philippines’ largest drugstore chain

![](https://jayagonoy.com/wp-content/uploads/1_Jvy5jDROfMUMQIF6CGrn2Q.png)

I didn’t know TGP was actually [established in the 1940s](https://tgp.com.ph/about-us), but I know that whenever I go, there’s a branch nearby. I mean, look: 1,900+ branches nationwide selling generic medicines for cheap. TGP The Generics Pharmacy has spawned a lot of competitors, but I still rely on it to give me the medicines I need.

After partnering with Robinsons Retail ([with the group as the majority stakeholder](http://business.inquirer.net/210379/robinsons-retail-buys-51-of-the-generics-pharmacy)), I saw a few noticeable results — aside from the inclusion of the Robinsons Retail brand in print ads, TGP has ditched its mortar-and-pestle logo and got a new typeface for its branding.

![](https://jayagonoy.com/wp-content/uploads/1_bWycdGkoDghnF4xwt1KrXg.png)

Also, I noticed that cross sign in the logo — I remember Southstar Drug’s rebrand a few years ago, and it has the same sign. Maybe this is part of Robinsons Retail’s branding strategy, but don’t take my word for it.

> TGP was the first to offer 100% generics while other drugstores sold 90% branded and only 10% generic medicine. In 2016, Robinsons Retail, through its subsidiary South Star Drug, Inc., acquired 51% of TGP. Todate, TGP has reached an unprecedented milestone with 1,900 stores all over Luzon, Visayas and Mindanao — the largest network of drugstores in the country.
> 
> **(**[**Source**](http://www.robinsonsretailholdings.com.ph/retail/the-generics-pharmacy)**)**

It’s not that complicated after all.
